"Change no behavior" is the entire definition


        
That repeating ✓ in the widget isn't decoration — it's the law. If the output changes, it isn't refactoring anymore; it's editing, and possibly breaking. Hold the boundaries clearly: refactoring is not a rewrite (tearing the kitchen down and rebuilding it), and it's not new features (adding dishes to the menu). It's strictly inside work — better names, less duplication, dead code gone, same behavior to the decimal.


        
Look at what each step actually bought you. Renaming x2 to hardbackCost made the code say what it means. Extracting the duplicated price-plus-wrap block into one helper is DRY in action — fix the pricing once, both line items get the fix. Deleting the commented-out "keep just in case" lines removed pure fog. Twelve lines became six. Output: identical.


        
Tests are the safety net


        
The obvious question: how do you tidy with confidence and know you broke nothing? You watched the answer fire after every step. That automatic "still returns $42 ✓" is a test — a tiny program that checks the real program. With tests in place, refactoring stops being scary: make a change, run the test, get a verdict in seconds.


        
No tests? Then you're rearranging the kitchen blindfolded and finding out on Saturday night. This is also why builders lean on version control: every cleanup step can be a save point, so even a botched step rolls back in one command. Net plus harness — that's how professionals tidy fearlessly, and why they do it weekly instead of "someday."


        
Why teams never stop doing this


        
Code rots — not by itself, but because requirements keep landing on it. Every shipped feature and every deadline shortcut leaves the kitchen a little messier than before. That accumulated mess has a name, technical debt, and it's the next lesson. Refactoring is how you pay it down: small payments on a schedule beat one desperate mega-clean a year too late.


        
The payoff compounds. The widget's final state — same output, half the lines, names a human can read — isn't just prettier. The next feature lands in that kitchen instead of the crime scene, which means it ships faster and breaks less. Mess is normal during service. Cleaning is what keeps service possible.
