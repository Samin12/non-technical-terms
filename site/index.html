Why this exists

I spent years as a software dev at Amazon. The hardest part of my job was never the code — it was the first six months of feeling stupid because everyone spoke in words I didn't have yet.

Now I build AI products and content full-time, and I watch smart, capable people bounce off tech for the same reason. Not because they can't think technically. Because nobody handed them the vocabulary. AI changed the game: you no longer need to write code to build things — but you do need the words, so you can ask better questions and know what you're asking for.

So this is Claude Club's dictionary: every term explained with an analogy you already understand, plus something interactive to play with — because pressing buttons beats memorizing definitions. Follow the Course Path one lesson at a time. By the end, you'll be dangerous enough to build.

And don't grind through it alone. The whole point of Claude Club is that you've got a room full of people on the same path — ask your "dumb" questions (they never are), join the live calls, and show off what you build along the way.
